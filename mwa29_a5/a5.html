Interactive Computer Graphics: Assignment 5

Viewing, Projection and Flat-Shading


Topics:

  • Parallel and Perspective Viewing
  • Lambertian diffuse reflection
  • Flat shading
  • triangle mesh models
  • Camera motion



  1. Write a program that loads a triangle mesh model (with at least 100 triangles) from an smf file. smf files can be found here

  2. Place at least two fixed white point light sources in your scene.

  3. Calculate a surface normal for each triangle.

  4. Calculate a diffuse (Lambertian) color for the triangle and assign the color to its three vertices. This will implement flat shading.

  5. Implement a rotating camera view of the model. Your camera should rotate on a cylinder around the model.
    Allow the user to interactively change the height, the orbit radius and speed of the camera.
    The camera should rotate around and look at the smf model (i.e. your LookAt point stays constant).
    The user should be able to start and stop the rotation of the camera.

  6. Allow the user to select either parallel or perspective projection.
